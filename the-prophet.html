بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ
اللَّهُمَّ صَلِّ وَسَلِّمْ عَلَىٰ نَبِيِّنَا مُحَمَّدٍ

The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ

Facts about his life, family, character, and mission — to know him is to love him.

مَنْ هُوَ ﷺ
Who He Was ﷺ
"Indeed, Allah conferred a great favour upon the believers when He sent among them a Messenger from themselves." — Quran 3:164
Full Name
Muhammad ibn Abdullah ibn Abdul-Muttalib ibn Hashim. He ﷺ was from the tribe of Quraysh, the most noble tribe among the Arabs, and from the clan of Banu Hashim.
Birth
Born on a Monday in the Year of the Elephant (approximately 570 CE) in Makkah. The exact date is a matter of scholarly discussion, though the 12th of Rabi' al-Awwal is widely cited.
Early Orphanhood
His father Abdullah passed away before he was born. His mother Aminah passed away when he was six years old. He was then cared for by his grandfather Abdul-Muttalib, and after his grandfather's death, by his uncle Abu Talib. Allah refers to this in the Quran:
أَلَمْ يَجِدْكَ يَتِيمًا فَآوَىٰ
"Did He not find you an orphan and give you refuge?" — Quran 93:6
Wet Nurse
He ﷺ was nursed by Halimah al-Sa'diyyah and spent his early years in the desert among the Banu Sa'd tribe, as was the custom among the Arabs for their children to grow up with strong Arabic and good health.
Known Before Prophethood As
Al-Sadiq (The Truthful) and Al-Amin (The Trustworthy). Even before revelation, the people of Makkah trusted him with their valuables and disputes.
His Names
The Prophet ﷺ said:
إِنَّ لِي أَسْمَاءً: أَنَا مُحَمَّدٌ، وَأَنَا أَحْمَدُ، وَأَنَا الْمَاحِي الَّذِي يَمْحُو اللَّهُ بِيَ الْكُفْرَ، وَأَنَا الْحَاشِرُ الَّذِي يُحْشَرُ النَّاسُ عَلَى قَدَمِي، وَأَنَا الْعَاقِبُ
"I have several names: I am Muhammad, I am Ahmad, I am al-Mahi (the Eraser) through whom Allah erases disbelief, I am al-Hashir (the Gatherer) at whose feet people will be gathered, and I am al-'Aqib (the Last — after whom there is no prophet)."
— Bukhari 3532; Muslim 2354
Lineage to Ibrahim (Abraham) عليه السلام
The Prophet ﷺ was a descendant of Prophet Isma'il, the son of Prophet Ibrahim عليه السلام. He ﷺ is the fulfilment of Ibrahim's supplication when he built the Ka'bah:
رَبَّنَا وَابْعَثْ فِيهِمْ رَسُولًا مِنْهُمْ
"Our Lord, and send among them a messenger from themselves." — Quran 2:129
أَهْلُ بَيْتِهِ ﷺ
His Family
He ﷺ was a devoted husband, a loving father, and a man who served his own household.
His Father
Abdullah ibn Abdul-Muttalib — died before the Prophet ﷺ was born, while on a trade journey. He was known for his good character among the Quraysh.
His Mother
Aminah bint Wahb — from the clan of Banu Zuhrah. She passed away when the Prophet ﷺ was about six years old, while returning from a visit to relatives in Madinah.
His Grandfather
Abdul-Muttalib ibn Hashim — the chief of Quraysh and guardian of the Ka'bah. He took care of the Prophet ﷺ after Aminah's death and loved him dearly. He passed away when the Prophet ﷺ was about eight years old.
His Uncle & Protector
Abu Talib — raised the Prophet ﷺ after Abdul-Muttalib's death and defended him fiercely throughout his life, even after prophethood, though he did not accept Islam himself.
His First Wife
Khadijah bint Khuwaylid رضي الله عنها — a successful businesswoman of Quraysh. She was the first to believe in him. He ﷺ married her when he was 25 and she was 40. She remained his only wife for 25 years until her passing. He ﷺ never stopped speaking of her with love. When asked about her, Aisha رضي الله عنها said the Prophet ﷺ would often mention Khadijah and praise her.
— Bukhari 3818
His Wives — Mothers of the Believers
After Khadijah's passing, the Prophet ﷺ married several women, each marriage carrying wisdom — caring for widows, strengthening alliances, or honouring companions. His wives include: Sawdah bint Zam'ah, Aisha bint Abi Bakr, Hafsah bint Umar, Zaynab bint Khuzaymah, Umm Salamah, Zaynab bint Jahsh, Juwayriyyah bint al-Harith, Umm Habibah, Safiyyah bint Huyayy, Maymunah bint al-Harith, and Maria al-Qibtiyyah رضي الله عنهن.
His Children
He ﷺ had seven children — all from Khadijah except Ibrahim:
Al-Qasim
His firstborn son. Passed away in infancy in Makkah.
Zaynab
His eldest daughter. Married Abu al-'As ibn al-Rabi'. She passed away in 8 AH.
Ruqayyah
Married Uthman ibn Affan رضي الله عنه. She passed away during the Battle of Badr.
Umm Kulthum
Also married Uthman ibn Affan رضي الله عنه after Ruqayyah's passing — which is why Uthman was called Dhun-Nurayn (possessor of two lights).
Fatimah
The most beloved of his daughters to him ﷺ. She married Ali ibn Abi Talib رضي الله عنه and was the mother of Hasan and Husayn. The Prophet ﷺ said she is the leader of the women of Paradise.
— Bukhari 3624
Abdullah
Also known as al-Tayyib and al-Tahir. Passed away in infancy.
Ibrahim
Born to Maria al-Qibtiyyah in Madinah. He passed away at around 16-18 months. The Prophet ﷺ wept and said: "The eyes shed tears and the heart grieves, but we say nothing except what pleases our Lord. Indeed, O Ibrahim, we are grieved by your departure."
— Bukhari 1303
A Husband at Home
When Aisha رضي الله عنها was asked what the Prophet ﷺ did at home, she said: "He used to be in the service of his family, and when the time for prayer came, he would go out to pray."
— Bukhari 676
صِفَتُهُ ﷺ
His Appearance
The Companions described him in vivid detail — and every description overflows with awe and love.
Overall Impression
The companion al-Bara' ibn 'Azib said: "The Prophet ﷺ was the most handsome of people, and the best of them in character. He was neither very tall nor short."
— Bukhari 3549; Muslim 2337
His Face
Abu Hurairah رضي الله عنه said: "I have never seen anything more beautiful than the Messenger of Allah ﷺ. It was as if the sun were flowing in his face."
— Tirmidhi (Shama'il) 12
His Build
He ﷺ was of medium height, broad-shouldered, with a wide chest. His limbs were large and strong. When he walked, he leaned slightly forward as if descending a slope.
— Tirmidhi (Shama'il)
His Hair
His hair was neither completely straight nor very curly. It sometimes reached his earlobes, and at times reached his shoulders. He ﷺ would sometimes part his hair.
— Bukhari 5905; Muslim 2338
His Eyes
He had large, dark eyes with natural kohl-like markings, long eyelashes, and a reddish tint in the whites of his eyes.
— Tirmidhi (Shama'il) 1
His Complexion
Anas ibn Malik رضي الله عنه described his skin as "neither extremely white nor very brown" — a luminous, fair complexion with a healthy glow.
— Bukhari 3547
His Smile
Abdullah ibn al-Harith said: "I have never seen anyone who smiled more than the Messenger of Allah ﷺ."
— Tirmidhi 3641
His Fragrance
Anas رضي الله عنه said: "I never smelled any musk or perfume more fragrant than the scent of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ."
— Muslim 2330
The Seal of Prophethood
Between his shoulder blades was a raised mark about the size of a pigeon's egg — the physical Seal of Prophethood, confirming he was the last of the prophets.
— Muslim 2346
أَخْلَاقُهُ ﷺ
His Character
وَإِنَّكَ لَعَلَىٰ خُلُقٍ عَظِيمٍ
"And indeed, you are of a great moral character." — Quran 68:4
When Aisha رضي الله عنها was asked about his character, she said: "His character was the Quran." — Muslim 746
His Mercy
When the people of Ta'if rejected him and sent their children to stone him until he bled, the Angel of the Mountains offered to crush them between the two mountains. He ﷺ refused and said: "Rather, I hope that Allah will bring from their descendants people who will worship Allah alone."
— Bukhari 3231; Muslim 1795
His Gentleness
Anas رضي الله عنه served the Prophet ﷺ for ten years and said: "He never said 'uff' to me, nor did he ever say to me about something I had done, 'Why did you do that?' or about something I had not done, 'Why didn't you do that?'"
— Bukhari 6038; Muslim 2309
His Humility
He ﷺ would mend his own shoes, sew his own clothes, and milk his own goat. He would sit with the poor, eat with them, and walk in the markets. A young girl could take him by the hand and lead him wherever she needed.
— Bukhari 2035; Ahmad 25662
His Generosity
Ibn Abbas رضي الله عنهما said: "The Messenger of Allah ﷺ was the most generous of people, and he was most generous during Ramadan. He was more generous than the blowing wind."
— Bukhari 6; Muslim 2308
His Bravery
Ali ibn Abi Talib رضي الله عنه said: "When the fighting intensified and eyes met eyes, we would seek protection behind the Messenger of Allah ﷺ. No one was closer to the enemy than he was."
— Ahmad 654
His Forgiveness
On the day of the Conquest of Makkah, he stood before the people of Quraysh — the same people who had persecuted him for 13 years, tried to kill him, and drove him from his home — and said: "Go, for you are free." He forgave them all.
— narrated in various seerah works; referenced in Bayhaqi
His Love for Children
He ﷺ would carry his grandsons Hasan and Husayn on his shoulders, kiss children, and let them play. A man saw the Prophet ﷺ kissing a child and said "I have ten children and I never kiss any of them." The Prophet ﷺ replied: "The one who does not show mercy will not be shown mercy."
— Bukhari 5997; Muslim 2318
His Concern for Others
He ﷺ would visit the sick, attend funerals, accept invitations from the poor, and would never look down upon anyone. He said: "None of you truly believes until he loves for his brother what he loves for himself."
— Bukhari 13; Muslim 45
He Never Sought Revenge for Himself
Aisha رضي الله عنها said: "The Messenger of Allah ﷺ never struck anything with his hand — neither a servant nor a woman — nor did he ever take revenge for himself. He would only become angry when the sacred limits of Allah were violated, and then he would take action for Allah's sake."
— Muslim 2328
نُبُوَّتُهُ ﷺ
His Prophethood
From the Cave of Hira to the farewell at Arafat — twenty-three years that changed the course of human history.
The First Revelation
At the age of 40, in the Cave of Hira on the Mountain of Light (Jabal al-Nur), the angel Jibril appeared to him and said "Read!" (Iqra'). He ﷺ replied, "I cannot read." Jibril squeezed him and repeated the command three times, then revealed the first verses:
اقْرَأْ بِاسْمِ رَبِّكَ الَّذِي خَلَقَ ۝ خَلَقَ الْإِنسَانَ مِنْ عَلَقٍ ۝ اقْرَأْ وَرَبُّكَ الْأَكْرَمُ
"Read in the name of your Lord who created. Created man from a clinging substance. Read, and your Lord is the Most Generous." — Quran 96:1-3
— Bukhari 3; Muslim 160
The First Believers
The first to believe among women was his wife Khadijah. The first among men was his closest friend Abu Bakr al-Siddiq. The first among children was Ali ibn Abi Talib. The first among freed slaves was Zayd ibn Harithah. These four stood by him when the rest of the world had not yet heard his message.
Years of Secret Da'wah
For the first three years, he ﷺ called people to Islam privately. The early Muslims would gather in the house of al-Arqam ibn Abi al-Arqam to learn and pray in secret, while Quraysh remained unaware of the growing community.
Persecution in Makkah
When the message became public, the Quraysh responded with severe persecution. Bilal was tortured under heavy stones in the desert heat. Sumayyah bint Khayyat became the first martyr in Islam. The Prophet ﷺ himself was mocked, had refuse thrown on him while praying, and was boycotted along with Banu Hashim for three years — during which they ate leaves from trees out of hunger.
The Year of Sorrow
In the 10th year of prophethood, both Khadijah and Abu Talib passed away within a short time of each other. He ﷺ lost his most beloved companion and his strongest protector in the same period — a year the scholars call 'Am al-Huzn (the Year of Sorrow).
Al-Isra' wal-Mi'raj — The Night Journey
In one of his most difficult periods, Allah honoured him with a miraculous night journey from Makkah to Jerusalem (al-Isra'), and then an ascension through the heavens (al-Mi'raj), where he met previous prophets, was shown Paradise and Hellfire, and received the command for five daily prayers.
سُبْحَانَ الَّذِي أَسْرَىٰ بِعَبْدِهِ لَيْلًا مِنَ الْمَسْجِدِ الْحَرَامِ إِلَى الْمَسْجِدِ الْأَقْصَى
"Exalted is He who took His servant by night from al-Masjid al-Haram to al-Masjid al-Aqsa." — Quran 17:1
The Hijrah — Migration to Madinah
In 622 CE, after 13 years in Makkah, Allah permitted the Prophet ﷺ and the Muslims to migrate to Madinah. He ﷺ left with Abu Bakr, hiding in the Cave of Thawr while the Quraysh searched for him. When Abu Bakr feared for their safety, the Prophet ﷺ said:
لَا تَحْزَنْ إِنَّ اللَّهَ مَعَنَا
"Do not grieve; indeed Allah is with us." — Quran 9:40
Building a Community in Madinah
In Madinah, he ﷺ built the first mosque (Masjid Quba' and then Masjid al-Nabawi), established brotherhood between the Muhajirun (migrants) and Ansar (helpers), drafted the Constitution of Madinah, and created a just society where Muslims, Jews, and others lived with defined rights and responsibilities.
Key Battles
The Prophet ﷺ participated in several battles in defence of the Muslim community. Among the most significant: Badr (2 AH) — the first decisive victory with just 313 Muslims; Uhud (3 AH) — a difficult day where he ﷺ was injured and 70 companions were martyred; Al-Khandaq/The Trench (5 AH) — the Muslims defended Madinah by digging a trench; and Hunayn (8 AH) — where he ﷺ stood firm when others fled.
The Treaty of Hudaybiyyah
In 6 AH, the Prophet ﷺ set out for Umrah but was blocked by the Quraysh. He agreed to a peace treaty that appeared unfavourable to the Muslims. Allah called it a clear victory. Within two years, the treaty led to more people entering Islam than in all the previous years combined.
إِنَّا فَتَحْنَا لَكَ فَتْحًا مُبِينًا
"Indeed, We have given you a clear conquest." — Quran 48:1
The Conquest of Makkah
In 8 AH, the Prophet ﷺ returned to Makkah with 10,000 companions. Makkah was taken without bloodshed. He entered humbly, his head lowered on his mount in gratitude to Allah. He cleansed the Ka'bah of all idols and declared: "Truth has come and falsehood has vanished. Indeed, falsehood is bound to vanish." (Quran 17:81)
The Farewell Sermon
In 10 AH, during his only Hajj, he ﷺ delivered his Farewell Sermon at Arafat before more than 100,000 people. He spoke of the sanctity of life and wealth, the rights of women, the abolition of racism, and the importance of holding on to the Quran. He ended by asking the people to pass the message on, saying the one who hears it may understand it better than the one who delivered it.
يَوْمُهُ ﷺ
His Daily Life
His daily life was a balance of worship, service, and kindness — simple in every way, yet extraordinary in its impact.
His Food
He ﷺ never ate his fill of bread for three consecutive days. His staple was dates and water. Months would pass without a fire being lit in his home for cooking. Aisha رضي الله عنها said: "The family of Muhammad ﷺ never ate their fill of wheat bread for two consecutive days."
— Bukhari 5416; Muslim 2970
His Bed
His bed was a leather mat stuffed with palm fibres. Umar رضي الله عنه once saw the marks of the mat on his side and wept. The Prophet ﷺ asked why, and Umar said the kings of Persia and Rome lived in luxury while the Messenger of Allah lived like this. He ﷺ replied: "Are you not satisfied that they have this world and we have the Hereafter?"
— Bukhari 4913; Muslim 1479
His Night Prayer
He ﷺ would stand in prayer at night until his feet would swell. When asked why he exerted himself so much when Allah had already forgiven his past and future, he replied: "Should I not be a grateful servant?"
— Bukhari 4837; Muslim 2820
His Manner of Speaking
He ﷺ spoke clearly and calmly. He would repeat important things three times to ensure understanding. He would face the person speaking to him fully. He never interrupted others, and his speech was precise — not too little, not too much.
— Bukhari 3567; Abu Dawud 4838
His Sitting
He ﷺ would sit wherever he found space, and would not have a designated seat that set him apart. Visitors who entered would not be able to tell which person in the gathering was the Prophet ﷺ because he did not elevate himself above others.
— Abu Dawud 4698
His Interactions
He ﷺ would greet everyone first, even children. He would not withdraw his hand from a handshake until the other person did. He would give full attention to whoever spoke to him, making them feel as though they were the most important person in the world.
— Tirmidhi 2490
His Humour
He ﷺ had a sense of humour but would only speak the truth even when joking. Once an old woman asked him to make dua that she enters Paradise. He said "Old women do not enter Paradise." She became upset, so he ﷺ clarified: "You will enter as a young woman" — referring to the people of Paradise being made youthful.
— Tirmidhi (Shama'il) 230
His Cleanliness
He ﷺ was particular about hygiene. He used the siwak (tooth stick) regularly, oiled his hair, wore perfume, and encouraged cleanliness as part of faith. He said: "Cleanliness is half of faith."
— Muslim 223
آخِرُ أَيَّامِهِ ﷺ
His Final Days
He ﷺ passed away having completed his mission, leaving behind a message that would endure until the Day of Judgement.
His Passing
The Prophet ﷺ passed away on Monday, 12th Rabi' al-Awwal, 11 AH (8th June 632 CE) in Madinah. He was 63 years old. He died in the room of Aisha رضي الله عنها, with his head resting on her lap.
His Last Words
In his final moments, he ﷺ raised his finger toward the sky and said:
اللَّهُمَّ الرَّفِيقَ الْأَعْلَىٰ
"O Allah, (I choose) the Highest Companion."
— Bukhari 4437; Muslim 2444
His Final Instructions
Among his last instructions to the Ummah: "The prayer, the prayer! And (treat well) those whom your right hands possess." He ﷺ also warned against taking graves as places of worship.
— Abu Dawud 5156; Bukhari 435
The Reaction of the Companions
When the news spread, Umar رضي الله عنه could not accept it and stood insisting the Prophet ﷺ had not died. Abu Bakr رضي الله عنه entered, kissed the Prophet's forehead, and addressed the people: "Whoever worshipped Muhammad, then Muhammad has died. But whoever worships Allah, then Allah is Ever-Living and shall never die." He then recited:
وَمَا مُحَمَّدٌ إِلَّا رَسُولٌ قَدْ خَلَتْ مِنْ قَبْلِهِ الرُّسُلُ
"Muhammad is no more than a messenger; other messengers have passed away before him." — Quran 3:144
— Bukhari 3668
Where He Was Buried
He ﷺ was buried in the same room in which he passed away — the room of Aisha رضي الله عنها — which is now enclosed within Masjid al-Nabawi in Madinah. Abu Bakr and Umar رضي الله عنهما were later buried beside him.
What He Left Behind
He ﷺ left behind no wealth, no palace, no kingdom. At the time of his death, his armour was mortgaged to a Jewish man for thirty measures of barley. He left behind the Quran and his Sunnah — a guidance for all of humanity until the end of time.
— Bukhari 2916
إِنَّ اللَّهَ وَمَلَائِكَتَهُ يُصَلُّونَ عَلَى النَّبِيِّ ۚ يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا صَلُّوا عَلَيْهِ وَسَلِّمُوا تَسْلِيمًا
"Indeed, Allah and His angels send blessings upon the Prophet. O you who believe, send blessings upon him and greet him with peace." — Quran 33:56
اللَّهُمَّ صَلِّ عَلَىٰ مُحَمَّدٍ وَعَلَىٰ آلِ مُحَمَّدٍ كَمَا صَلَّيْتَ عَلَىٰ إِبْرَاهِيمَ وَعَلَىٰ آلِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ إِنَّكَ حَمِيدٌ مَجِيدٌ
"O Allah, send blessings upon Muhammad and upon the family of Muhammad, as You sent blessings upon Ibrahim and the family of Ibrahim. Indeed, You are Praiseworthy, Glorious."
